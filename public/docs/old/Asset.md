# Asset Management (Business Overview)

The **Asset Management module** manages all physical and operational assets within the organization across their full lifecycle.

It ensures that assets are:
- Properly registered
- Maintained regularly
- Used efficiently
- Tracked until disposal

Asset operations are governed using **Workflow**, initiated through **Requests**, executed via **Tasks**, versioned where applicable, and fully recorded in the **Audit Trail**.

---

## Why This Matters

In a manufacturing environment:

- Machines and equipment are critical to operations
- Breakdowns cause production loss
- Poor tracking leads to inefficiencies
- Compliance requires maintenance and usage records

Without proper control:
- Maintenance may be missed
- Unauthorized changes may occur
- Asset history may be incomplete
- Audit readiness is weakened

This module ensures the organization always knows:

✅ What assets exist  
✅ Where they are  
✅ What condition they are in  
✅ When maintenance is due  
✅ Who is responsible  

---

## Key Objectives

The Asset module ensures:

- Full visibility of all assets
- Controlled changes to asset records
- Planned and traceable maintenance
- Reduced downtime
- Support for audits and compliance

---

## Key Components

### 1. Asset (Identity)

An **Asset** represents a physical or operational item owned or used by the organization.

Examples:
- Machines
- Tools
- Vehicles
- IT equipment

✅ Each asset defines:
- Asset ID
- Name and type
- Location
- Owning OrgTeam
- Operational status

✅ The Asset represents **identity**, not historical state  
✅ Changes are tracked via **Workflow and Audit**

---

### 2. Asset Lifecycle (Controlled Process)

Assets move through a controlled lifecycle:

1. Registration (creation)
2. Operation (active use)
3. Maintenance (planned or corrective)
4. Retirement / Disposal

✅ Lifecycle transitions are governed by **Workflow**  
✅ Ownership and approvals are clearly defined

---

### 3. Requests (Initiation)

Most asset activities begin with a **Request**.

Examples:
- Maintenance request
- Relocation request
- Decommission request
- Upgrade or modification request

✅ A Request:
- Captures the need
- Triggers the appropriate workflow
- Ensures traceability from initiation to completion

---

### 4. Workflow (Control & Approval)

Asset-related activities are governed by **Workflows** owned by an **OrgTeam**.

✅ Workflow controls:
- Registration approval
- Maintenance authorization
- Asset modification
- Disposal approval

✅ Typical workflow steps:
- Submitted
- Reviewed
- Approved
- Executed
- Completed

✅ No critical asset change bypasses workflow

---

### 5. Tasks & Work Orders (Execution)

Actual work is executed using **Tasks**.

Examples:
- Inspect machine
- Perform maintenance
- Replace component
- Verify asset condition

✅ Tasks:
- Are generated by workflow steps
- Are assigned to roles or technicians
- Track execution and completion

✅ Work Orders are implemented as structured Task Lists

---

### 6. Versioning (Controlled Changes)

Versioning is applied where asset information must be **controlled and historically traceable**.

Examples:
- Asset specifications
- Maintenance procedures
- Calibration standards
- Operating instructions linked to assets

✅ Versioning ensures:
- Changes are intentional
- Old definitions are preserved
- Only approved versions are used

✅ Version lifecycle: Draft → Approved → Published → Archived


---

### 7. Asset Status

Shows the current operational state of the asset:

- Active
- Under Maintenance
- Out of Service
- Disposed / Obsolete

✅ Status changes are:
- Workflow-controlled
- Logged in the Audit Trail

---

### 8. Audit Trail (Traceability)

All asset-related actions are recorded in the **central Audit Trail**.

Examples:
- Asset created
- Asset updated
- Maintenance approved
- Maintenance completed
- Asset disposed

✅ Audit records include:
- Who performed the action
- What changed
- When it happened
- Outcome and reason

✅ Essential for audits and investigations

---

## How It Works (Controlled Flow)

1. Asset is registered
2. Ownership assigned to an OrgTeam
3. Requests initiate asset actions
4. Workflow governs approvals
5. Tasks execute work
6. Status is updated
7. History is recorded in Audit Trail
8. Asset is eventually retired or disposed

✅ Full lifecycle is controlled and traceable

---

## Example (Simple Scenario)

**Asset:** CNC Machine  
**Location:** Production Floor  
**Owning OrgTeam:** Maintenance Team  

Process:
1. CNC Machine is registered
2. Monthly maintenance schedule exists
3. Operator submits maintenance request
4. Workflow approves maintenance
5. Task List is generated
6. Technician performs maintenance
7. Status and history are updated
8. Audit trail records all actions

✅ Machine remains compliant and operational
